Privacy Compliance

What is Privacy Compliance?

Privacy Compliance means following laws that protect people's personal information. These laws tell you how to collect, store, use, and share customer data responsibly and legally.

Getting privacy wrong can result in hefty fines, legal troubles, and loss of customer trust. But done right, it becomes a competitive advantage that shows customers you take their privacy seriously.

Key Privacy Laws for Australian Businesses

  • Privacy Act 1988 (Australian Privacy Principles): Applies to businesses with annual turnover of $3 million or more, or any business handling health information or credit reporting.
  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR): European law that applies if you have customers in the EU, regardless of where your business is located.
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA): Applies if you have customers in California and meet certain revenue or data processing thresholds.
  • Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) Scheme: Australian law requiring you to report serious data breaches to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner and affected individuals.

Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)

  • APP 1 - Open and Transparent Management: Have a clearly written privacy policy on your website explaining how you handle personal information.
  • APP 3 - Collection of Solicited Personal Information: Only collect personal information you actually need for your business purposes.
  • APP 5 - Notification of Collection: Tell people when you're collecting their information and why you need it.
  • APP 6 - Use or Disclosure: Only use personal information for the purposes you collected it, unless you have consent or it's legally required.
  • APP 11 - Security of Personal Information: Take reasonable steps to protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access.
  • APP 12 - Access to Personal Information: Give people access to their personal information when they request it.
  • APP 13 - Correction of Personal Information: Fix personal information when people show you it's wrong or out of date.

Practical Privacy Compliance Steps

  • Data Mapping: Create a simple spreadsheet listing what personal information you collect, where you store it, who has access, and how long you keep it.
  • Privacy Policy: Write a clear privacy policy using plain English. Include what information you collect, why you collect it, how you use it, and who you share it with.
  • Consent Management: Ensure you have proper consent for collecting and using personal information. Keep records of when and how consent was given.
  • Data Security: Implement technical safeguards like encryption, access controls, and secure storage. Follow our other security guides for detailed implementation.
  • Breach Response: Develop procedures for responding to data breaches, including notification requirements and timelines.
  • Staff Training: Train employees on privacy requirements and their responsibilities when handling personal information.

Notifiable Data Breaches Scheme

  • What triggers notification: Any breach likely to result in serious harm to affected individuals must be reported within 72 hours.
  • Who to notify: Report to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) and notify affected individuals as soon as practicable.
  • What to include: Description of the breach, types of information involved, recommendations for individuals to protect themselves.
  • How to report: Use the OAIC's online data breach notification form or call their helpline for guidance.
  • Record keeping: Keep detailed records of all data breaches, even those that don't require notification, for at least two years.

Simple Steps to Start Today

  • ☐ Check if your business meets the Privacy Act thresholds (annual turnover over $3M or handling health information)
  • ☐ Download our data mapping template and list all personal information your business collects and stores
  • ☐ Review your website's privacy policy or create one using the OAIC's privacy policy template
  • ☐ Audit your data storage locations and implement access controls to limit who can see personal information
  • ☐ Sign up for OAIC privacy alerts and guidance updates
  • ☐ Create a data breach response checklist including OAIC contact details
  • ☐ Schedule annual privacy compliance reviews to ensure ongoing compliance
