Risk Assessment

What is a Risk Assessment?

A Risk Assessment helps you identify what could go wrong with your business's cybersecurity and how badly it could hurt you. It's like doing a security inspection of your business to find the weak spots before criminals do.

By understanding your biggest risks, you can focus your limited time and money on protecting what matters most to your business.

Types of Risk Assessment

  • Qualitative Risk Assessment: Uses descriptive terms like "high," "medium," and "low" to rate risks. Easier for small businesses without complex data.
  • Quantitative Risk Assessment: Uses numbers and calculations to estimate financial impact. More precise but requires detailed data most small businesses don't have.
  • Hybrid Risk Assessment: Combines both approaches. Uses numbers where available and descriptive terms elsewhere.

Most small businesses should start with a qualitative assessment and add quantitative elements as they gather more data.

Risk Assessment Process

  • Asset Identification: List everything valuable in your business including data, systems, and processes. Use our asset inventory template to get started.
  • Threat Identification: Identify what could attack or damage your assets. Common threats include ransomware, phishing, insider threats, and natural disasters.
  • Vulnerability Assessment: Find weaknesses that threats could exploit. Check for unpatched software, weak passwords, and missing security controls.
  • Impact Analysis: Determine how much damage each successful attack could cause. Consider financial loss, operational disruption, and reputation damage.
  • Likelihood Assessment: Estimate how likely each threat is to succeed. Base this on industry data and your current security posture.
  • Risk Calculation: Combine impact and likelihood to determine overall risk level for each scenario.

Simple Risk Rating System

  • Impact Levels: Low (minimal business disruption), Medium (some disruption but manageable), High (significant disruption or major financial loss)
  • Likelihood Levels: Low (unlikely to happen), Medium (could happen), High (likely to happen)
  • Risk Levels: Critical (High Impact + High Likelihood), High (High Impact + Medium Likelihood OR Medium Impact + High Likelihood), Medium (Medium Impact + Medium Likelihood), Low (Low Impact regardless of likelihood)
  • Priority Actions: Critical risks need immediate action, High risks need action within 30 days, Medium risks within 90 days, Low risks when resources allow

Simple Steps to Start Today

  • ☐ Download our risk assessment template and asset inventory worksheet
  • ☐ List your five most valuable business assets (data, systems, processes)
  • ☐ Identify the three most likely threats to each asset
  • ☐ Rate the potential impact and likelihood using our simple Low/Medium/High scale
  • ☐ Calculate risk levels and prioritise the Critical and High risks for immediate action
  • ☐ Document current security controls protecting each asset
  • ☐ Schedule quarterly risk assessment reviews to update your findings
