Understanding Cyber Risk

Why This Matters

Before you can protect your business, you need to understand what you're protecting it from. Cyber risk isn't just about technology - it's about understanding how threats could impact your business and making informed decisions about where to invest your limited time and resources.

Small businesses are targeted 43% more than large companies because criminals know you likely have fewer resources to defend yourself. But understanding risk gives you the power to make smart security decisions.

The Risk Formula

Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact

This simple formula helps you evaluate any security decision. High risk areas need immediate attention, while low risk areas can wait.

Breaking Down the Formula

Threat

What could go wrong? Common threats include ransomware, phishing emails, data breaches, system failures, or disgruntled employees. The likelihood varies by industry and business size.

Vulnerability

What weaknesses exist? Outdated software, weak passwords, lack of backups, untrained staff, or poor physical security. These are gaps that threats can exploit.

Impact

What would happen if this threat exploited this vulnerability? Consider financial loss, business downtime, reputation damage, legal consequences, and recovery costs.

Real-World Example

Scenario: Ransomware Attack

Threat: Ransomware gangs targeting small businesses (High - happening daily)

Vulnerability: Staff clicking phishing links + no backup system (High - very common)

Impact: Business shut down for weeks, $50,000 ransom demand, customer data lost (Very High)

Risk Level: CRITICAL - Needs immediate action

Your First Risk Assessment

Ask yourself these questions about your business:

  • ✓ What would happen if our computers were locked by ransomware tomorrow?
  • ✓ How would we operate if our main systems were down for a week?
  • ✓ What's our most valuable data, and where is it stored?
  • ✓ Who has access to our critical systems and data?
  • ✓ When did we last update our software and systems?
  • ✓ Have we ever tested our backup and recovery procedures?

Common High-Risk Areas for Small Business

Based on thousands of incidents, these areas typically represent the highest risk for small businesses:

  • Email-based attacks - 94% of malware arrives via email
  • Weak or reused passwords - 80% of breaches involve weak credentials
  • Unpatched software - Criminals exploit known vulnerabilities
  • Lack of backups - No way to recover from ransomware or hardware failure
  • Untrained staff - Humans are often the weakest link
