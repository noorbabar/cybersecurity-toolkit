Types of Attacks & Data

Why Care?

Cyber criminals target small businesses because they often have weaker protections. Knowing how they attack helps you stop them.

Common Attacks and What To Do

Phishing (Fake Emails)

Cyber criminals send fake emails pretending to be someone you trust. They want you to click links or give your password.

How to stay safe:

  1. Check the sender: If you don’t know the person or company, don’t open the email.
  2. Don’t click links or download attachments: If you’re unsure, don’t click, hover your mouse over links to see the real website address.
  3. Verify requests: If the email asks for passwords or money, call the company or person directly using a known phone number (not one in the email).
  4. Delete suspicious emails immediately.

Ransomware (Locked Files)

A virus that locks your files and demands money to unlock them.

How to stay safe:

  1. Backup your files regularly: Save copies of important files to a USB stick or free cloud storage like Google Drive or OneDrive.
  2. Ask for help: If you don’t know how to do backups, ask a trusted tech person or friend to set it up for you.
  3. Keep your computer updated: When your computer asks to install updates, say “yes” to keep it secure.
  4. Don’t pay ransom: If infected, contact a cybersecurity expert or law enforcement.

Data Breaches (Stolen Info)

Cyber criminals steal customer info, payment details, or private business data.

How to stay safe:

  1. Create strong passwords: Use at least 8 characters, mixing uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols (e.g. P@ssw0rd1!).
  2. Use a password manager: Download a free app like LastPass or Bitwarden to create and store passwords securely.
  3. Use different passwords: Don’t use the same password for different accounts.
  4. Enable two-step verification: Turn on “2FA” or “multi-factor authentication” on accounts that offer it, you’ll get a code on your phone when logging in.

Insider Problems

Staff or helpers might accidentally or purposely cause problems.

How to stay safe:

  1. Limit access: Only give staff access to files or systems they need for their job.
  2. Change passwords when someone leaves: If an employee or contractor leaves, change all passwords they knew immediately.
  3. Keep a list: Keep a list of who has access to what, and review it regularly.

System Problems & Mistakes

Computers can crash or people can make mistakes that cause lost data.

How to stay safe:

  1. Backup important files: Save copies on a USB stick or cloud storage regularly, once a week is a good start.
  2. Ask for help: If you don’t know how to backup or update your software, ask a tech-savvy friend or service.
  3. Keep software updated: When your computer or apps ask to update, say “yes”, updates fix problems and add security.

What Do Cyber Criminals Want?

They want information they can use or sell to make money or cause damage:

  • Customer names and contact info
  • Bank account and payment details
  • Passwords and login information
  • Business secrets, pricing, or contracts

Start Today: Simple Actions

  • ✓ Don’t open emails from unknown people
  • ✓ Save copies of important files to USB or cloud
  • ✓ Use a password manager app to create strong, different passwords
  • ✓ Turn on two-step verification (2FA) on your important accounts
  • ✓ Keep your computer and apps updated when prompted
  • ✓ Limit who can access your important data
  • ✓ Ask a trusted friend or IT service to help if unsure
