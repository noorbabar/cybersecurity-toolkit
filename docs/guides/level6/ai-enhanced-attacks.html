AI Enhanced Attacks

What are AI Enhanced Attacks?

AI Enhanced Attacks use artificial intelligence to make cyber attacks more convincing, targeted, and harder to detect. Criminals are using the same AI tools that help businesses to create more sophisticated scams and break through traditional security defenses.

These attacks are becoming more common and effective because AI can automate complex tasks that previously required skilled hackers, making advanced attacks accessible to more criminals.

Common Types of AI Enhanced Attacks

  • Deepfake Voice Calls: AI-generated voice recordings that sound exactly like someone you know, used to trick you into transferring money or revealing sensitive information.
  • AI-Generated Phishing Emails: Perfectly written emails with no spelling errors that mimic your suppliers, banks, or colleagues to steal login credentials.
  • Automated Social Engineering: AI chatbots that can hold convincing conversations to extract information or convince you to take harmful actions.
  • Personalized Spear Phishing: AI analyses your social media and public information to create highly targeted attacks that reference your interests and relationships.
  • AI-Powered Password Attacks: Machine learning algorithms that can guess passwords more effectively by learning from previous breaches and personal information.
  • Adaptive Malware: Malicious software that uses AI to change its behavior to avoid detection by antivirus programs.

Red Flags of AI Enhanced Attacks

  • Urgent voice calls: Calls requesting immediate money transfers or sensitive information, especially if the caller claims to be someone you know but sounds slightly different.
  • Perfect grammar in unexpected emails: Phishing emails that are unusually well-written compared to typical spam, especially from foreign scammers.
  • Highly personalised requests: Messages that reference specific details about your business, relationships, or recent activities found online.
  • Unusual behavioral patterns: Colleagues or suppliers acting differently in digital communications, asking for information they should already have.
  • Pressure tactics: Requests that create artificial urgency to prevent you from verifying the request through normal channels.
  • Multiple attack vectors: Coordinated attacks across email, phone, and social media happening simultaneously.

Defending Against AI Enhanced Attacks

  • Implement verification protocols: Always verify unusual requests through a separate communication channel. If someone calls requesting urgent action, hang up and call them back on a known number.
  • Limit public information: Review your social media privacy settings and business information online. Reduce what attackers can learn about you and your business.
  • Use advanced email security: Enable AI-powered email protection services like Microsoft Defender for Office 365 or Google Workspace security features.
  • Train staff on AI threats: Educate your team about deepfakes, AI-generated content, and new verification procedures. Practice spotting AI-enhanced attacks.
  • Implement behavioral monitoring: Use security tools that detect unusual login patterns, data access, or system behavior that might indicate compromise.
  • Multi-factor authentication everywhere: See our Identity & Access Management guide for comprehensive MFA implementation.

Creating AI Attack Response Procedures

  • Establish verification codes: Create secret words or phrases with key suppliers, banks, and team members for phone verification of unusual requests.
  • Set financial limits: Require multiple approvals for payments above certain amounts, and never waive this requirement regardless of urgency.
  • Document suspicious contacts: Keep records of suspected AI-enhanced attacks to help identify patterns and improve your defenses.
  • Regular communication drills: Practice your verification procedures with staff and key contacts so everyone knows what to expect.
  • Update incident response plans: Include AI-enhanced attacks in your incident response procedures and train staff on the specific steps to take.
  • Monitor for deepfakes: Check if your voice or image has been used in deepfake content by searching for your name and unusual media appearances.

Real World Example: AI Voice Scam Targets CEO

Case: A Melbourne manufacturing company's finance manager received a call from someone who sounded exactly like the CEO, requesting an urgent international transfer for a confidential acquisition.

AI Attack: Scammers had used publicly available audio from company videos to create a convincing deepfake voice.

Prevention: The finance manager followed company protocol and asked for the verification code word, which the caller couldn't provide.

Result: The attempted $85,000 fraud was prevented because the company had established verification procedures for unusual financial requests.

Simple Steps to Start Today

  • ☐ Establish verification code words with your bank, key suppliers, and team members for telephone confirmations
  • ☐ Review and tighten privacy settings on all business social media accounts
  • ☐ Enable advanced email security features in your Office 365 or Google Workspace admin console
  • ☐ Create a policy requiring secondary verification for all financial transfers above $1,000
  • ☐ Train your team to recognise AI-generated content and verify unexpected requests
  • ☐ Set up Google Alerts for your business name and key personnel to monitor for misuse
  • ☐ Document any suspected AI-enhanced attack attempts for pattern analysis
