Third Party Security

What is Third Party Security?

Third Party Security means checking that companies you work with won't accidentally expose your business data or let hackers in through their systems.

Think of it like this: if your accountant gets hacked, the criminals might access your financial data too. If your IT company gets breached, they could use their access to attack your systems.

Who Are Your Third Parties?

  • Your accountant or bookkeeper
  • IT support company
  • Cloud storage providers (Dropbox, Google Drive)
  • Your lawyer
  • Cleaning company
  • Anyone else who has your business data or can access your building

Simple Risk Check

Ask yourself these questions about each company you work with:

  • Do they have my customer data?
  • Can they access my computer systems?
  • Do they come into my office?
  • Would my business be in trouble if they got hacked?

If you answered yes to any of these, you need to check their security.

What to Ask Them

For important suppliers, ask these simple questions:

  • "Do you have cyber insurance?" If they say no, that's a red flag.
  • "What happens if you get hacked?" They should have a plan and promise to tell you immediately.
  • "How do you protect my data?" They should mention encryption and secure storage.
  • "Who can see my information?" Fewer people is better.

What to Do Right Now

  • Make a list: Write down every company that has your data or system access
  • Check contracts: Make sure they promise to tell you about security problems within 24 hours
  • Remove old access: If you stopped using a service, make sure they deleted your data
  • Use separate accounts: Never give suppliers your main business login details
  • Check annually: Once a year, review who has access and remove anyone you don't need

Real World Example: Accountant Gets Hacked

What happened: A small Sydney business had their financial data stolen when their accounting firm got hacked.

The problem: The accountant didn't have proper security and never told the business about the breach for three weeks.

The fix: Now they only work with accountants who have cyber insurance and promise immediate breach notification.

Simple Steps to Start Today

  • ☐ Write down everyone who has your business data
  • ☐ Email your top 5 suppliers asking if they have cyber insurance
  • ☐ Check if old suppliers still have access to your accounts
  • ☐ Create separate login accounts for any suppliers who need system access
  • ☐ Add "breach notification within 24 hours" to your standard contract terms
