Data Backup & Recovery

Why Backup Your Data?

Backing up your data means making extra copies of your important files and information so you don’t lose everything if your computer crashes, gets stolen, or ransomware locks you out.

It’s one of the simplest ways to protect your business and keep running even when things go wrong.

How to Back Up Effectively

  • Choose what to back up: Focus on critical data like customer info, financial records, and important documents.
  • Back up regularly: Weekly backups are the minimum. For busy businesses, daily is best.
  • Use multiple backup locations: Store backups both in the cloud (Google Drive, Dropbox) and on an external hard drive kept separate from your computer.
  • Test your backups: Occasionally check that you can restore files from your backup.
  • Keep backup software up to date: This ensures the process works smoothly and securely.

Example: When Backups Save the Day

Case: In 2019 the City of Baltimore was hit by ransomware, locking many systems and stopping key services. Limited backups meant recovery took weeks and cost over $18 million.

Impact: Services were disrupted, payments delayed, and public trust was damaged.

Fix: Baltimore has since improved backup and recovery plans to prevent future disasters.

Source: 2019 Baltimore Ransomware Attack

Start Backing Up This Week

  • ☐ Identify your most important data to back up
  • ☐ Set up automatic cloud backups
  • ☐ Buy or use an external hard drive for extra backups
  • ☐ Test restoring a file from your backup

Having backups means you can recover quickly from mistakes, crashes, or attacks.
